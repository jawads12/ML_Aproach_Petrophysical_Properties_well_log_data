Abstract 
Reservoir char acterization is a process to predict the key properties of a 
reservoir which highlights the production feasibility of a field. Apart from this 
reservoir characterization helps engineers to understand underground rock 
formations to optimize hydrocarbon prod uction. Reservoir key properties can 
be predicted by interpreting the well log data. Conventional methods are 
mainly based on empirical models and manual analysis which can be time 
consuming. It can also provide inaccurate predictions. However, with 
advancements in machine learning (ML), data-driven approaches have emerged 
as powerful tools for predicting reservoir properties accurately. This study 
focuses on the application of different types of machine learning models for 
predicting key reservoir properti es. By key reservoir properties, we are 
referring to porosity, permeability and water saturation. Several well logs data 
are used as the input parameters for this study . After, training the models, the 
research focuses on the comparison of those ML models to check which one of 
them provides the most accurate outcome. The models are then validated and 
evaluated to evaluate their performance. This study is based on the Titas Gas 
Field located in Bangladesh. Well log s data of well no 10 and 11 of Titas Gas 
field were collected, preprocessed and are used to train the ML models . The 
study not only bridges the gap between data science and petroleum engineering 
but also opens new avenues for research and industrial applications in 
hydrocarbon exploration and production from Bangladesh’s perspective.

Chapter 1: INTRODUCTION 
Reservoir characterization is defined as the process of evaluating underground 
rock formations to determine their potential for storing and transmitting 
hydrocarbons. It is a key aspect to understand whether the formation is capable 
enough to produce hydrocarbons at a feasible rate. It is certainly a critical part 
of the exploration and production stage of a reservoir. 
 
 By reservoir characterization, we basically refe r to major properties of a 
formation such as porosity, permeability and water Saturation. Porosity is 
defined as the available space to hold fluids in a rock whereas permeability 
refers to how easily fluids can move in a rock. The water saturation, on the 
other hand, refers to the amount of oil, gas and water in a pore space. These 
properties help engineers to determine reservoir's storage capacity and to 
understand t he flow behavior of the fluid.  Those key properties of the 
formation can be obtained from w ell log data as it is the detailed record of rock 
properties. Thus, well -log interpretation can provide many major properties of 
a reservoir which are essential for optimizing production strategies. The 
method of well log interpretation and reservoir chara cterization can be divided 
into two sectors - Conventional approach and Advanced data-driven approach. 
 
This study focuses on the Titas Gas Field, one of the largest producing gas fields 
in Bangladesh. It is operated by Bangladesh Gas Fields Company Limite d 
(BGFCL), located near the bank of Titas river at Bramonbaria. Despite of its 
contribution to the national energy sector, detailed petro -physical 
characterization using modern data -driven approaches remains limited. In this 
research, well log data from we ll no 10 and 11 of the Titas field are being 
used for detailed analysis of predicting porosity, permeability, and water 
saturation from conventional well log data by machine learning models.

1.1 BACKGROUND 
 
For thousands of years, the energy demand has made the civilization innovate 
many techniques and methods to extract hydrocarbons efficiently within a 
sufficient amount of time. However, those conventional interpretations can be 
time consuming and provide less efficiency in results as they are based on 
manual analysis, empirical correlation and mathematical models. Conventional 
method thus not only consumes more time but also causes wrong assumptions 
during production stage. However, with the increasing availability of well log 
data and advancements in artif icial intelligence, machine learning (ML) has 
emerged as a powerful tool for reservoir characterization. 
Machine learning algorithms can not only identify complex patterns within 
large datasets but also find the missing key points in conventional methods. 
 
There are many supervised machine learning models such as Random Forest, 
Gradient Boosting, Neural Networks etc. Those models can be trained with 
historical well log data to predict the properties with high accuracy. These 
models use the data -driven appro ach which reduces reliance on manual 
interpretation and improves prediction accuracy. This can lead to better 
decision-making in field development, production optimization and 
hydrocarbon recovery.

2.3.1.2 Outliers Treatment 
 
Outlier in dataset is major concern in developing machine learning models. 
Outliers influence the model to learn the unrealistic pattern. Thus it is removed 
before the data splitting process. There are mainly two methods are used for 
this purpose. One is Interquartile Range (IQR)  method and another is Z -score 
method. In this study, IQR method was implemented to remove outliers. 
 
Interquartile Range (IQR) method 
 
IQR method is a widely used statistical technique for detecting outliers in 
numerical d atasets. It is based on the spread of the middle 50% of data, 
calculated as the difference between the third quartile (Q3) or 75% of dataset 
and the first quartile (Q1)  or 25% of dataset . Q3 and Q1 then used to calculate 
the IQR value. This IQR value is used to determine lower limit and upper limit.

2.3.1.3 Duplicate Value Treatment 
 
If a value repeated more  than once it is considered as a duplicate value. Lower 
percentage of duplicate value is not a concerning thing, but higher percentage 
of duplicate value can alter the prediction efficiency. Duplicated data can be 
eliminated simply using the Pandas library. 
 
 
2.3.1.4 Noisy Data Treatment  
 
Noisy data are mainly unrealistic, irrelevant or error dataset. High degree of 
noisy dataset can affect the performance of predictive model. There are several 
methods to eliminate the noisy value. In this study, Binning m ethod is used to 
conduct the outlier treatment.  
 
Binning Method  
 
Binning is a data pre -processing technique used to convert continuous 
numerical variables into discrete intervals or categories, often referred to as 
"bins." This method helps reduce the ef fects of minor observation errors and 
handle noisy data by grouping similar values together. Binning can be 
performed using techniques such as equal-width binning , where the range of 
data is divided into intervals of the same size, or equal-frequency binni ng, 
where each bin contains the same number of data points.

usually from 1 to 0. This done in order to ensure that there is no dataset with 
larger numerical value influencing  the model while predicting.  One of the 
common methods used to data normalization is Min-Max scaling method.

2.3.3 Feature Selection 
 
Feature selection is a very crucial step, as it  involves identifying and retaining 
the most relevant input variables for predictive model. The goal is to reduce 
dimensionality, improve model performance, and enhance interpretability by 
eliminating redundant, irrelevant, or noisy features. Visualizing the input 
feature using Heat-map correlation is a common method. 
 
Heat-map Correlation  
 
Heat-map based feature selection works by visualizing the correlation matrix, 
which shows the pairwise correlation coefficients between features and the 
target variable. Features that exhibit a strong positive or negative correlation 
with the target are considered relevant and are selected, while those with low or 
zero correlation are considered less useful and may be excluded.

2.4 PREVIOUS STUDIES  
 
2.4.1 Recent studies  
 
 Hadavimoghaddam et al. (2021) used machine learning algorithms s uch 
as XGboost, LightGBM, AdaBoost, CatBoost and Super Learner to 
predict water saturation data from well log data. Among those 
algorithms XGBoost and Super Learner were the most efficient ones as 
those algorithms predicted water saturation without relying  on 
resistivity log data.[1] 
 
 
 Zhang et al. (2022) developed machine learning models such as K-nearest 
neighbors (KNN), Support vector regression (SVR) and Random Forest 
(RF) and LightGBM based on mathematical linear or nonlinear 
relationship between well logs petrophysical properties such as porosity, 
permeability and water saturation. Among them, LightGBM produced 
more reliable results. To enhance the predicting quality and reliability, a 
hybrid algorithm named CRBM -Bayes-LightGBM was introduced to 
predict porosity permeability and water saturation.[2] 
 
 Cheddad (2023) trained three machine learning algorithms, namely 
Artificial Neural Network (ANN), Random Forest Classifier (RFC), and 
Support Vector Machine (SVM) to predict permeability logs from 
conventional logs and compare the models. The performance of the 
models is comparable, with a slight advantage observed for the ANN 
model in terms of performance.[3] 
 
 Nugroho et al. (2024) used Random Forest and XGBoosting algorithms to 
predict effective porosity a nd permeability. The results of the prediction 
model were then optimized by GridSearchCV (GS). XGBoosting was 
found to be the most effective one in predicting porosity while Random 
Forest provided better effectiveness with permeability.[4] 
 
 Smith et al. (2 024) utilized a combination of well log interpretation and 
various machine learning models to evaluate reservoir properties. At the

3.4 WELL LOGS PARAMETER 
 
3.4.1 Input Parameters 
 
The following well log parameters will serve as the primary input param eters 
for training the machine learning models to predict reservoir properties: 
 
 
Depth 
 
The Depth log is the foundational measurement, acting as the vertical ruler for 
all other data points. It precisely indicates how far down into the Earth's crust 
each measurement was taken 
 
Gamma Ray Log (GR) 
 
The Gamma Ray (GR) log is like a natural radioactivity scanner for rocks. It 
measures the natural gamma radiation emitted by formations, which is 
typically higher in clay -rich rocks due to their potassium, uranium , and 
thorium content 
. 
Caliper Log (CALI) 
 
The Caliper (CALI) log provides a direct measurement of the wellbore's actual 
diameter. It helps engineers assess the borehole's condition, identify zones of 
instability, and ensure accurate interpretation of other logs. 
 
Spontaneous Potential Log (SP) 
 
The Spontaneous Potential (SP) log detects natural electrical currents generated 
by the interaction between drilling mud  and formation fluids. It acts as an 
indicator for identifying permeable zones and estimating their shale content 


--- PAGE 33 ---
 
Chapter 3: MATERIALS AND METHODOLOGY 19 
 
 
 
Spherically Focused Log - Shallow (SFLU) 
 
The SFLU (Spherically Focused Log - Shallow) is a resistivity log designed to 
measure the electrical resistance of the formation  at a relatively shallow depth. 
It helps differentiate between zones containing resistive hydrocarbons and 
conductive water in the invaded zone near the wellbore 
 
Micro-spherically Focused Log (MSFL) 
 
The MSFL (Micro -spherically Focused Log)  is an even shallower resistivity 
measurement than SFLU, providing a very clos e-up view of the electrical 
properties right at the borehole wall within the invaded zone. 
 
Induction Log - Deep (ILD) 
 
The ILD is a crucial resistivity log that measures the electrical resistance of the 
formation far beyond the drilling mud's invaded zone , reaching into the 
undisturbed reservoir. ILD log is a primary indicator for identifying 
hydrocarbon-bearing zones and estimating their saturation. 
 
Bulk Density Log (RHOB) 
 
The Bulk Density (RHOB)  log measures the electron density of the rock, which 
is then converted into its bulk density. This log is fundamental for identifying 
different rock types and also used in calculating the rock's porosity. 
 
Density Correction Log (DRHO) 
 
The Density Correction (DRHO) log is a quality control measure for the RHOB 
log. A significant DRHO value alerts engineers that the RHOB reading might be 
compromised, prompting careful consideration or correction for more reliable 
porosity calculations. 
 
Photoelectric Factor Log (PEF) 


--- PAGE 34 ---
 
Chapter 3: MATERIALS AND METHODOLOGY 20 
 
The Photoelectric Factor (PEF)  log measures t he photoelectric absorption index 
of the formation, a property highly sensitive to the rock's mineral composition. 
Different mi nerals have distinct PEF values, allowing geologists to precisely 
determine the lithology of the formation. 
 
Sonic Transit Time Log (DT) 
 
The Sonic Transit Time (DT) log measures the time it takes for a sound wave to 
travel through a specific distance of rock . This log is primarily used to calculate 
sonic porosity. 
 
Z-Axis Density Log (ZDEN) 
 
The Z-Axis Density (ZDEN)  log is essenti ally another term used for the Bulk 
Density (RHOB) measurement. It refers to the density measurement taken along 
the vertical axis of the logging tool 
 
Neutron Porosity Log (NPHI) 
 
The Neutron Porosity (NPHI) log measures the hydrogen content within the 
formation, which is directly proportional to the amount of fluid in the pore 
spaces. Therefore, it's a direct measure of the rock's porosity 
 
Density Porosity Log (DPOR) 
 
The Density Porosity (DPHI) log  is a calculated porosity value derived directly 
from the Bulk Density (RHOB)  log. It is a fundamental method for quantifying 
the volume of void space within the reservoir rock. 
 
Shorter Normal Resistivity Log (SN) 
 
The Shorter Normal Resistivity (SN) log is another type of resistivity 
measurement, generally pr oviding a relatively shallow depth of investigation 
compared to the deep induction logs. It helps characterize the electrical 
resistance of the invaded zone, where drilling mud filtrate has displaced some 
of the original formation fluids

3.4.2 Target Variables  
 
The machine learning models will be trained  to predict the following  reservoir 
properties: 
 
Derived Porosity (DPHI) 
 
Derived porosity  is the porosity value which has derived from logs or 
calculations considering all the other factors, such as NP HI, RHOB and DRHO. 
It is also known as effective porosity. Effective p orosity indicates the capacity of 
the reservoir to store fluids. It  is fundamental for permeability and water 
saturation modeling. 
 
 
 Water Saturation (SW) 
 
SW indicates the fraction of pore space of rock which is  filled with water. It’s 
crucial for reservoir evaluation. It helps in differentiate  between water and 
hydrocarbons zone. 
 
PERM (Permeability) 
 
Permeability measures the ability of the rock to transmit fluids. It is highly 
influenced by porosity and pore connectivity. Predicting permeability can be 
difficult as it fluctuates quite often. Thus we can encounter with lower 
prediction accuracy.

3.7.2 Support Vector Machine Models 
Support Vector Machine (SVM) is a supervised machine learning algorithm 
used for both classification and regression tasks. It works by finding the optimal 
hyper-plane that bes t separates data points of different classes or predicts 
continuous values with minimal error. 
 
Support Vector Regression (SVR) 
SVR is an extension of Support Vector Machines (SVM) for regression tasks. It 
attempts to fit a function within an epsilon-insensitive tube, ensuring that errors 
within this margin are ignored. The objective is to find a hyperplane that 
minimizes deviation beyond this tube. SVR can behave as a linear or nonlinear 
model depending on the choice of kernel (e.g., linear, polynomial, RBF).

Random Forest Regressor 
Random Forest is an ensemble technique that builds multiple decision trees 
using boot strapped samples of the data and aggregates their outputs. Each tree 
is trained on a random subset of features, which helps to reduce overfitting and 
improve generalization. The final prediction is the average of predictions from 
all trees in the forest. 
 
3.7.4 Gradient Boosting Models 
Gradient boosting models construct an ensemble of weak learners (typically 
decision trees) in a sequential manner. Each new tree attempts to correct the 
residual errors of the previous ensemble using gradient descent optimization. 
XGBoost (Extreme Gradient Boosting) 
XGBoost is a high-performance gradient boosting library that introduces 
regularization and advanced optimization techniques such as second-order 
gradient approximation, shrinkage, and column sampling. It is known for its 
speed and predictive power, especially in structured/tabular data. 
 
3.7.5 Deep Network Model 
Neural networks are flexible, data-driven models capable of learning both 
linear and highly nonlinear relationships. 
Deep Neural Network  
Neural network is a type of feed forward neural network consisting of input, 
hidden, and output layers. Each neuron computes a weighted sum of its inputs 
followed by a nonlinear activation function. The network is trained using back 
propagation and gradient descent to minimize prediction error. MLPs are 
powerful function approximators, especially effective when sufficient data is 
available.

5.8 Limitations, Opportunities, and Reliability of ML Approach 
 
Machine learning models used in this study, can predict porosity, permeability 
and water saturation at certain degree of accuracy. However there are some 
limitations of this process as well. Th is section discusses the limit ation, 
opportunities and reliability of this study. 
 
5.8.1 Limitation 
Limitations of the machine learning approach are given below: 
 ML models require large volumes of high quality data to predict with 
high degree accuracy. But similarly to many real-world reservoirs, Titas 
Gas Field well log  data may be incomplete, noisy and full of missing 
data, especially for permeability and water saturation. 
 
 In many formations permeability fluctuates at very high degree, thus 
predicting permeability in some formation may be difficult with those 
models. 
 
 Due to geological heterogeneity, the model may not be generalized for 
other field as well. 
 
 Without careful pre-processing and validation, models may overfit to 
training data, reducing the effectiveness of the prediction 
 
 Core Data was absent from the dataset, thus the study has to rely on 
empirical equations . The assumption taken to utilize those equation, 
sometime mismatched from real reservoir condition 
 
 The study was focused on only two wells of Titas Gas Field, for better 
generalization, data from multiple well is required. 


--- PAGE 82 ---
 
Chapter 5: DISCUSSION 68 
 
 Based on only five models, other models such as CatBoost, LIghtGBM, 
Decision Tree, ANN etc. can be implemented as well. 
 
5.8.2 Opportunities of ML Approach 
There are several opportunities of machine learning approach. These are 
discussed in the following section: 
 
 Machine learning models can predict the target variables of all well 
across a formation, once it is trained. 
 The estimation using machine learning models are quite fast and reliable. 
 ML models can detect non -linear relationships as well as complex 
patterns among the various parameters of the formation. 
 It is cost effective and can be used for real time application. 
 The models reduce the human error s, enhancing more automation 
approach. 
 ML models can combine core data, well log data, seismic data, 
production data to improve accuracy and robustness. 
 
 With some advanced models, 90% or more than that accuracy achieved. 
 
5.8.3 Reliability  
 
Considering both  of the limitations and opportunities  of machine learning 
approach, the Random Forest model  can be effective tool s to predict res ervoir 
characteristics, as it achieved high degree of accuracy . Bu t this accuracy may 
vary according to the characteristics of the formation. Each formation has its 
unique characteristics, thus even though predicting properties using machine 
learning is fast and cost effective process, but no major decision should be tak en 
based on solely machine learning models. Instead they can be used as a 
complementary tool alongside the traditional conventional methods.

6.1 Conclusion 
In this study, five different machine learning models  of different classes  were 
used to predi ct three major reservoir properties, which are porosity, 
permeability and water saturation. Random forest outperformed all the other 
models to predict all parameters  with highest accuracy followed by Deep 
Neural Network  and Xgboost respectively. For permea bility and porosity 
prediction Random Forest  outperformed rest of the models. Deep Neural 
Network achieved highest accuracy in water saturation prediction. Xgboost can 
also be used as the alternatives of Random Forest. 
 
This study not only shows the most effective model for reservoir prediction, but 
also shows that models like Linear regression, Support vector regression should 
not be used as predictive model for reservoir characterization as they achieved 
very low degree of accuracy. This is obvious as those models are based on linear 
relationship between parameters. But in real time reservoir, the parameters 
maintain a non -linear relationship with one another.  Models like Random 
forest, Xgboost, Deep Neural Network  can handle the non -linear relationship, 
thus those achieved higher accuracy. Also permeability of a formation depends 
less on well logs parameters, thus lower accuracy was achieved here by all 
models. Having core data, could have influenced the permeability prediction 
even better. Even though wit h Random Forest, XGBoost and Deep Neural 
Network achieved 90-95% accuracy, but the limitation of the study and 
reliability of the models should also be considered. Thus it is recommended to 
use those models as a complementary tool alongside the traditional methods. 
 
6.2 Recommendation for the Further Study 
In t his study, gradient models like  Xgboost, L inear models like Linear 
Regression, Support Vector Matrix models like Support Vector Regression, 
decision tree assemble based models like Random Forest and deep learning 
model like Deep Neural Network were used.  Even though, four different types
